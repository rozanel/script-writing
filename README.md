# UNTITLED — Short Film Screenplay

## The Core
An old man named Posy engineers a weekend at his estate with the children of his estranged siblings  because he has been alone in this house for forty years and this is his last chance to feel like family again. What begins as an act of guilt and manipulation slowly becomes something none of them expected.

## Characters

**The Cousins**
- **Vichara (26)** — Daughter of Vicheka. Self-made, sharp, observant. Has been building her life alone so long she's forgotten what belonging feels like.
- **Norith (25)** — Son of Bopha. Warm, loud, impossible to dislike. His mother's cooking lives in his hands even when he doesn't know it.
- **Amra (19)** — Daughter of Visot. Photography student. The one who touched the cloth. Carries more self-doubt than she shows.
- **Mony (18)** — Son of Visot. The golden child. Precise, methodical, quietly emotional. Was at the museum with Amra.

**The Outsider**
- **Vivoth (30)** — Son of Sophea, Posy's former maid. Raised on Posy's support. Knows everything. Orchestrated the entire setup. Feels more than he lets on.

**The Old Man**
- **Posy** — Mysterious on first appearance. Commanding, quietly warm in small ways. Separated from his siblings during wartime. Has been waiting in this house for forty years for something to come back to him. His arc is the quiet one.

## Story Context
Posy's siblings — Visot, Bopha, and Vicheka — were sent away during the war. They survived. They built separate lives. Their children grew up not knowing their parents had siblings. Posy, running out of time, hires Vivoth to bring them all together without revealing why. What unfolds over one weekend slowly dismantles forty years of silence.

## Thematic Core
- What a family owes each other across silence and distance
- The difference between blood and belonging
- What it means to keep something ( a room, a pen, a cracked spoon ) for forty years
